It's time to refurbish, the old king said;
The crenellations are crumbled and worn,
The towers have tumbled, buttresses shorn,
No space remains for a spike nor a head.
Forgive me my lord, the architect's dead;
Not three days gone, his widow still forlorn,
Tributes and wreaths flock in - his tomb adorn,
His children wonder how they will be fed.
'Tis sad, said the king, when a good man dies
Ere his work on the Earth is yet complete.
His hand was quick and his eye was so keen,
But so it is when the twinkling of eyes
Breeds betrayal and later spawns deceit;
He should ne'er have gone to bed with the queen!

-JG
